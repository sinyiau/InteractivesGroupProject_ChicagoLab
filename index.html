CIT Officer Presence Incidents Between Mental Health and Overall UoF Incidents

CIT Officer Presence in UoF Incidents Over Time

Methodology


○	TRR data was combined with CIT training data which included:
    ■	Officer name
    ■	Officer star number
    ■	Date of CIT certification
○	Each TRR report was coded (1,0) to indicate whether the reporting officer had CIT training before the event date
○	A separate table was created to deduplicate UoF events (multiple TRR reports are often made for the same event)
○	Each event was coded (1,0) for whether or not a CIT trained officer was involved in the response
○	Each event was additionally coded (1,0) for its relation to a mental health issue
○	Additional columns were added to capture the event date as well as several other criteria for exploratory data analysis (Beat, TRR Type, Subject Injury)
○	For the pie charts the total percentage of CIT trained officer responses to mental health and overall UoF incidents were calculated accordingly:
    ■	COUNTIFS([CIT Trained Officer?],1,[Mental Health],1)/COUNTIF([Mental Health],1)
    ■	COUNTIF(CIT Trained Officer?,1]/COUNT([Year])
○	For the volume over time chart, the proportion of UoF incidents with CIT trained officers present for each year were calculated accordingly:
    ■	COUNTIFS([Year],year,[CIT Trained Officer?],1,[Mental Health?],1)/COUNTIFS([Year],year,[Mental Health?],1)
    ■	COUNTIFS([Year],year,[CIT Trained Officer?],1)/COUNTIF([Year],year)

How Mental Health Calls Are Understood in Chicago

How Mental Health Calls Are Answered in Chicago

For this visualization, the call data was pivoted on the mental health scenarios in the final dispatch. This reduced the total calls to 22,279 and divided them into 8 categories. The final dispatch labelled ‘unauth absence’ has been expanded to the label ‘unauthorized absence’ in the final visualization.

Chicago's Response to Mental Health Emergency Calls

The third visualization was pivoted on final disposition codes used by the responders. The disposition codes were filtered for the Z in the alphanumeric code. Sankey diagram of mental health calls

Methodology

1. There are five types of initial dispatches, after discussion with Kari, we decided to eliminate check wellbeing calls. Therefore, this chart is done based on the 22,279 mental health 911 calls in Chicago in 2024.

2. I created the chart aiming to show how these calls went from initial dispatch to final dispatch, and finally to final disposition. I filtered each type of initial dispatch, then used pivot tables to get the number of final dispatches.

3. Kari explained to us that all disposition codes ending in Z are mental health-related. Since the data came like 0520Z - ASSAULT AGG KNIFE, I split text into columns and filtered out non-mental health disposition codes. From here, I sorted the Final Dispatch: mental health related, non-mental health related, 988 calls and Cares&alternatives. The reason why I put 988 calls and Cares&alternatives separately is because they are mental health only service.

4. The pivot tables for final disposition were very long because there are different codes under the same type, for example, 15IZ - Inhalator;MenHlth;Ho and 15PZ - Inhalator;MenHlth;Ot. Many of these codes only orrcured once or twice. Therefore, I screened out code types with more than 10 counts. Most of the disposition codes could be confirmed, but one abbreviation was confusing: Other Mis (disposition code 19.)

5. For the chart, as there are a total of three stages, I checked how the flourish sankey chart sample data sheet worked. Then I divided the type of calls into source and destination, and marked step 0 to step 1 (initial dispatch to final dispatch) and step 1 to step 2 (final dispatch to final disposition.)
